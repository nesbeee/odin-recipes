Crispy Fried KangKong


Description

This Crispy Fried Kangkong, also known as Kangkong Crisp, is a beautiful crispy recipe for water spinach leaves. This simple, inexpensive, and incredibly tasty Filipino appetizer or snack is ideal for any gathering or celebration.

This recipe is straightforward, yet simple but nutritious. It only requires a small amount of time and effort to produce this recipe. This dish is a healthier alternative to chicharon (pork rinds), so if you enjoy them, you might want to give it a try with this vegetable crispy alternative.

  • 2 bunches kangkong (water spinach) leaves
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 - 1/2 cup water (ice-cold)
  • 1 piece fresh egg
  • cooking oil

Steps

  1. Separate the leaves of the kangkong from the stem.
  2. Wash and clean the kangkong leaves thoroughly under running water to remove dirt. Then wipe it to dry.
  3. Dredge the kangkong leaves individually in cornstarch and set them aside.
  4. Make the batter. Combine flour, cornstarch, baking powder, egg, salt and pepper. Pour in ice cold water and mix well. Set aside.
  5. Heat up enough cooking oil in a frying pan over medium-low heat.
  6. When the oil is hot enough, dip the kangkong leaves in the prepared batter one after the other. Then fry until golden and crisp, about 1 to 2 minutes. Note: Repeat the same process or steps until all the kangkong leaves are cooked.
  7. Remove from the pan, let the oil drain, then transfer to a serving plate lined with paper towels.
  8. Serve it crisp with your favorite dipping sauce.
